Creating Quests for MMORPG
I worked during the summer vacation of 2018 as a game design intern for Revelation Online Mobile, an MMORPG developed by Pangu Studio of NetEase. Under supervision of the lead designer and a system designer mentor, I created several side quests focusing on the narrative and gameplay that would make players feel immersed in the high fantasy world.
time summer 2018
platform Unity
work quest design, level design
Methodology
Before setting off on the adventure, it is very important to have an overview of what the game ‘feels like’ and how we should deliver side quests. Revelation's backstory is pretty much like the extension of traditional Chinese legend: Classic of Mountains and Seas (Shanhaijing), a high fantasy world with many imaginary creatures. This means a large scale of possibility to implement new gameplay under a more conservative theme.
Since the game has already gone through 5 iteration cycle by the time I joined, I was able to concentrate on design with a fairly complete system framework. But before that, as a quest design working in a system design team, I needed to figure out how Relevation's systems work.
system overview
System in MMORPG context refers to the framework that takes charge of a certain gameplay. Revelation contains a bunch of systems ranging from very basic ones such Character Growth, Equipment, quest (just as other MMORPGs do) to more specific ones like Adventure, Transformation and Camera. The following are those that I mainly worked on.
I mostly worked in creating content for Adventure System, which provides unique experience to amplify the feeling of "freedom" and "depth". It aims to encourage players to explore the world by providing engaging single-player focused experience. Quests in Adventure System are not compulsory and are designed for those who like to spend more time in game.
Transformation System allows character to transform into another creature in the game by replacing their models, animations, skills, even the faction. For instance, if a character becomes a crab, they walk sideways, use claws to attack, and become friends with other AI crabs. The system grants designers brand new ways to think about how living things in the game interact with each other, and inspires me to implement an unforgettable experience.
Camera System is not a rare one in MMO games, but the one in Revelation has some cool features such as capturing ghost creatures (that are unseen normally) and recognizing the name of anything in the frame.
Player can collect some sound and use a special item to play sounds thanks to Audio System. It is special in a way that it records the sound ID thus allowing triggering events that have sounds as parameters, which means more options to choose from when designing side quests.
design principle
My mentor and I both agree that the most fun comes from the crosspoint of multiple system, in other word, combining systems. There are 4 special ones listed above, and of course many basic systems (economy, battle, quest, achievement) are also open to offer fundamental gameplay.
After getting familiar with systems, I figured out a pratical way to create quests: observe the components of gameplay and try finding some possible options for each. The formula is:
Interactive Feature * Entity * Fantasy * Reward = Complete quest.
Interactive feature comes from the functions that game system could offer. Entity is the subject (usually the player) and object of the interaction. Fantasy is the narrative logic behind the interaction: why is it happening and why the player should take the challenge? And reward is of course, what you will get.
Players need feedback and reward for exploring Adventure System and doing optional quests. We decided to use a unique currency (called "Rainbow Jade") only for buying items in Adventure System so it won't affect in-game economic balance and is easier to determine the price of items.
Production
When it comes to production, my major work was filling sets of spreadsheets to implement quest logic and adjusting things directly in Unity. I created side quests, did rounds of debugging and iteration, and helped organize playtests with naive guests.
ideation
Before I joined, the team already had some prototype design for Adventure System. After playing all of them, I talked with my mentor on the feeling of disconnection between the quests and the world. Many quests only uses one specific system feature just to show that feature, not to serve the world's narrative. This made me feel I am playing a collection of mini games, each game was fun but they actually didn't help the players know more about the world.
direction of improvement
From the former experience and a "all-for-one" faith, I managed to figure out what could be the correct direction of fixing the problem and creating new side quests. For the old ones, I wanted to find ways to put them in a larger context (to fit the backstory, to combine more systems, to be more challening and rewarding) so the core fun of Adventure System is well reflected in those quests. For the new ones, I stick to the design principles concluded in Methodology session.
typical workflow
The majority of "design" work is done with the combination of several spreadsheets each of which implements a system in the game. Generally to create a quest, I will need to modify quite a few number of spreadsheets. Also, as the tool programmers have developed in-engine plugins that allows the direct control of in-game entity, I was able to place NPC, their patrol routes (if applicable) and modify a small scale of terrain.
quest examples
Crab Wars
Side quest: Crab Wars asks players to transform into crabs and go through a bizarre experience joining in a fight of crabs. This quest fully uses the Transform System and is possible with faction switching feature.
Quest Procedure: 1. Character (as human) notices some blue crabs fighting with red ones. 2. If the character uses transformation tool to transform into a crab with either color, they can talk to the crab commander and choose to fight for their species. 3. After killing some enemy crabs the character can talk to the commander again to finish the quest and get reward from crab's gratitude.
The interesting part in gameplay is that the player could actually do quest 2 times for the both team, and have different conversation and receive achievement. I think this quest does a good job in encouraging players to explore the world, and crabs fighting is an interesting and rare point so transforming and getting a quest meets player's curiosity.
Unseen Love
Side quest: Unseen Love tells a story between a woman and her ghost lover featuring Camera System and branching narratives.
Quest Procedure: 1. Character finds a weeping woman standing under a peach tree. 2. As the character talks to the woman, she says that she's just missing her husband who set off fishing a month ago and never came back. She presumes he's already dead but can't see a fact. The quest is not formally given out but the player may search for an answer. 3. If the character moves the camera towards the tree, they will find a ghost man under the peach tree, looking at the woman. The ghost says that he's been dead for some time and even his ghost figure is fading away, and his last wish is that the woman may live happy without him. 4. If the character takes a photo of the tree and shows to the woman, the woman get relieved as she knows now he's always here with her even though she cannot see him in the picture.
I spent some time researching the love story between human and ghost and designed this quest using the Camera System that allows the ghost to appear in photo frame. I wanted to use dialogue clues as indirect control for those who are eager to interact with the world. The most surprising moment should be turning on the camera to see a person right in front of you, and branching narratives give everyone a chance to choose the story they want.

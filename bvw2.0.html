Building Virtual Worlds
Building Virtual Worlds is a class for the first semester of Master of Entertainment Technology Program during which I in a team of five take part as game designer and artist to make quick game prototypes. The process takes one to three weeks.
Water Truckin': Evil Hero
Time: One week.
Theme: Fun.
Platform: Jam-O-Drum.
Role: Created environmental art and UI, coded island collapse animation.

Gameplay Video
DESIGN ELEMENT
Asymmetrical role. Add fun and replayable things in game. One player drives the firetruck, another one controls the water cannon. By assigning different abilities we force players to stay connected to each other and communicate along the game.
Unavoidable conflict. It is supposed to be a fast paced game no longer than 2min each round. The battleground gets smaller as the game goes on, players will have to fight more intensely. Reasonable randomness. For competitive games people are expecting each round to be different (and the result as well). It’s not good to be 100% skill or 100% luck, so adding a little randomness will help the game gets fun. The randomness must be well controlled to not overwhelm players’ performance.
Visual Instructions. Tell the players what will happen in game and make them understand the situation.
THINGS TO WORK ON
Clearer instructions. There is almost no tutorial so we have to tell players manually how to play and win the game.
Balance. Most players claim that driving is actually harder than spraying water. It is not a bad thing to have a diverse job difficulty inside a team, but we need to clarify it in the first place or players might get frustrated for underperforming.
Promposal
Time: Two Weeks.
Theme: Interactive Story.
Platform: Oculus Rift S.
Role: Created the story, designed interaction, modeled and animated all characters.
Gameplay Video
DESIGN ELEMENT
An easy story with ups and downs. It will be a 4min experience including interaction time so do not make the story too complicated. We go for a classroom romance since everyone probably has some experience on that. And we think adding a little surprise in story would help with the emotional flow.
Meaningful interactions. We learn from What Remains of Edith Finch combining interactions with story. We leave important moments waiting to be accomplished by player interaction so they feel being in the situation rather than watching a story sequence.
THINGS TO WORK ON
Delivering the interaction more clearly. Some players have a hard time figuring out how the story and interaction is connected. And it is hard to have the same feeling as What Remains of Edith Finch as we are short on art and sound resources. So it might be better if we deliver the interaction in a clearer way with less metaphors.
More playtesting. More playtesters help more in finding bugs and testing the interactions. If they don’t understand, that is probably we are making it too complicated.
Paramania
Time: Three Weeks.
Theme: ETC Festival.
Platform: CAVE (cave automatic virtual environment).
Role: Designed all puzzles, created environmental art, coordinated playtests.
Gameplay Video
DESIGN ELEMENT
Think of the hardware. We are putting players into a very special game environment where they are surrounded by three large screens so they are like ‘inside’ the game world. This is an excellent chance for us to make a fully immersive game so we start the exploration concepting a Egytian pyramid where adventurers go and find treasures by solving puzzles.
Be aware of experience flow. At the early stage the game merely consists of multiple puzzles with little transition between each other, which for most players could be tedious. I then suggested that we have pre-scripted animations between two puzzles so the experience will have its relaxed and intense part. That is also how we implement story into the actual game. [PICTURE jiaocha]
Unified theme. At first we have many ideas about puzzles and they all seem to work in some way. But after several playtests we find that have just one unified theme for all puzzles will make it clearer for players and easier to control the difficulty for designers. We agreed on using ‘light’ as our core element as it is COOL and uses CAVE environment in a smart way. [PICTURE]
THINGS TO WORK ON
Be aware of experience for the whole process. We have some really nice puzzles and our environment really has Egyptian feeling, but that doesn’t work very well if the experience is way too long. We have a 9 min playthrough which exceeds the 6 min expectation much. It turns out we still need more playtests as some players may need more time to understand and play with the mechanics.

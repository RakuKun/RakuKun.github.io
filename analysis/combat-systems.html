Combat Systems: Three Games as Examples (WIP)
Of all types of game fantasies, brawling with enemies controlling fictional characters has really stood out and pushed the envelope of many new game mechanics. Melee combat systems are regarded as a core part of a considerable amount of games with lots of supporting gameplay systems revolving around it. This article will break down the combat systems of three typical action games: Bloodborne, the new God of War, and Monster Hunter: World.
time spring 2021
Overview
Analyzing the combat system in any of contemporary action games feels like a daunting task. I find it useful in many times to apply the MDA Framework that starts with breaking down the verbs of in-game actions that convey certain degree of feelings, and then goes deeper on combat system's components, and ultimately the base level of how everything is integrated into a complete system.

In this section I'll be mainly using the examples of the three games mentioned above, not only because I'm most familiar with these games, and they represent different ways to approach the same goal: make the combat system fun and challenging.
defining success
Many great action games feature its combat system as a fundamental selling point. However, nowadays many players are being more aware of the "integration" of the game, reminding us developers the importance of making everything fit in. That's to say, a good combat system should be in harmony of the game's other systems that altogether creates the illusion of being in the game world.
verbs and feelings
difficulty curve
Just like the soul series made by From Software, the level of Bloodborne is beautiful and challenging as well. Waking up in a vast world with few hints for where to go next already is already harsh for any players, let alone there are numbers of monsters along the way that can kill you in three or four blows.
The way Bloodborne does the tutorial is a quite linear map at the beginning where enemies are deadly but also easy to deal with. And whatever players do, they will eventually come to a stage where they need to face the first storyline boss. It is almost unavoidable that many deaths are to come, but that will be a very important lesson for players to accept failures and get familiar with the map. It is just fair that players use life - usually not very valuable in the early game - to learn some basic rules and routines. As the story moves on, the game offers more options to choose from and as players have better equipment, higher level and more combat experience, it will not be so overwhelming. The game in this way ask players in a polite way to think carefully and choose a route they feel comfortable, leaving space to choose for both new players and veterans.
level's functions
The levels have functions. One of the key things in Bloodborne's map is the shortcut which drastically reduces the time needed to travel between places. This can have several positive results.
First, from a feedback perspective, shortcut could ease the negative feeling of death because once opened the players don't have to fight unnecessary monsters, they can just speedrun.
Second, from a goal perspective, shortcuts serve as small goals for players in their run. Due to the difficulty most people cannot finish bosses in one shot, so opening a shortcut whenever entering a new area becomes a preferable choice for players. This design as well in the experience aspect intrigues player's curiosity and keeps their eyes open.
Third, From Software decompose the large world into smaller areas and use shortcuts to connect them all, making them feel larger than they really are. Doors, hidden ladders, stairways just make Bloodborne a real 3-dimensional world connecting the lower and higher places.
Last but not least, shortcuts are also event triggers for dynamically loading map. It takes several seconds to play the open door animation, leaving time to load the map of next area.
3-dimensional world
Bloodborne makes players "WOW" when they finish a long journey and climb a ladder just to find themselves right where they wake up! What is more, developers still retain control of the level design as the actions the character can do are relatively limited, for instance, they can only take a leap forward after dashing. So designers can predict where players are going. In this way, the level design work is highly goal-oriented and risk-controlled.
being gothic
I believe that art style should serve the game settings and storytelling. The gothic, triangular tops of buildings help establish navigational landmarks which is friendly to players in a misty world. lamps' warm color and the reflection of moonlight on the ground also serves as path indicator.
system design
combat system
In the game, Gothic buildings, people's clothing and firearms indicate that Bloodborne is based on a Victorian world, a time of technology innovation and city lores. Bloodborne offers a much faster combat pace for both player and enemy and its combat system is high risk & high reward.
Unlike Soul Series, in Bloodborne there is only a type of wood shield available for blocking, so in most cases people do parry and dodge to avoid taking damage, which is more time-sensitive and feels more intense. Pistol parry, a very effective way to make enemies stunned and leave space for high-damage visceral attacking, needs to be done very precisely at the moment enemies attack, or the enemy will use their attacks to punish the player harshly.
Another very interesting setting is the Rally mechanism. It is a risk/reward combat mechanic that after being hit by an enemy, players may jump back in the action and attack the opponent to quickly refill any lost health before the health bar actually depletes the amount. The tricky thing is that any follow-up attacks you take will negate the chance to regain the former losing health. So timing is crucial to the execution of this mechanic. This setting really challenges the players as attacking back boldly but also avoiding getting another hit is a hard lesson for everyone.
There is an item especially designed to increase the intensity of fighting: Beast Blood Pellet, which when used boosts player's beast status. Beast status increases physical damage and damage taken as well, in order to keep the damage boost you have to continue attacking so the meter doesn't fall. Due to this design, attacking in beasthood really has a feeling of someone slowly turning into a blood-thirsty beast.
All the designs in combat system share a same clear goal: to promote a much more aggressive style for combat where the meaning of Bloodborne is revealed: We encounter powerful enemies, get killed by them, kill them back, and finally the hunter outwears are stained with blood.
Insight
"The Insight stat represents the depth of inhuman knowledge. Needed to ring special bell, but induces frenzy."
Insight is a key attribute which affects lots of in-game events and player's other attributes, and I really want to talk about it since it is the core setting of Bloodborne. Basically the higher Insight character possesses, the more they are exposed to inhuman knowledge, and the more this world reveals itself. If Blood Echo is the currency to buy more for the character, Insight is the currency for knowing about Bloodborne's world.
One example: when Insight reaches 40, Lesser Amygdala become visible in the Cathedral Ward and other areas. The game uses such explicit way to demonstrate Insight's power: it is really affecting how the character and player observes the world, far beyond changing some attribute's value.
Balancing
Bloodborne has very stylized From Software's way of storytelling and narrative design.
story
world
"The Night, and the Dream, were long..."
Even till today, there is no universally acknowledged explanation for even all three endings. One reason is Bloodborne's world setting is highly fantastic and hybrid. Nevertheless, people do agree on the fact that its backstory is constructed upon Cthulhu Mythos but with much unique setting. This game takes place in Yharnam, a city plagued diseases, blood craze and death. The disease in the city transforms human victims into bestial creatures. The player acts as one of the Hunters and hunts down those horrifying creatures while exploring the buried backstory of Yharnam.
theme
In my perspective, the theme of Bloodborne, if there must be just one, is mankind's evolution.
We could analyze its theme by looking into three endings which is all related to a secret endboss, the Moon Presence.
1. In "Yharnam Sunrise", the character "lives the hunt" and awakens in real world where Yharnam embraces a new morning. This is the easiest ending as there is no need to fight any boss further than Mergo's Wetnurse. The character finishes the mission assigned and makes a fair deal with the watcher of the hunter's dream and leaves. Everything is back on track as if nothing really happened during that night.
2. In "Honouring Wishes", the character is "captivated by the Moon Presence" and takes over Gehrman's place to "watch over the hunter's dream". This ending shows the condition where the character wants to fight against the current situation and get rid of someone else's control, but they are just not strong enough to finish it and become the next miserable watcher.
3. In "Childhood's Beginning", the character defeats the Moon Presence and "became an infant Great One, lifting humanity into its next childhood". This end is only possible after the character has consumed at least three pieces of One Third of Umbilical Cord which gives human the power of Great Ones.
As the description tells, the three endings are basically stats that identifies the character's (and player's) acknowledge of the essence of this world and the willingness to change it.
narrative design
storytelling
Bloodborne's story is hard to understand partly because of its way of storytelling. Bloodborne never "tells" the details of a story. For most times, it gives player a start and a goal, and do the rest of storytelling sorely in environment, dialogues, and item descriptions. One must be very patient and careful not to omit the little evidence of the possible existence of backstory. And it only gives fragments of the story, so the work of putting fragmented information together into a story is left to players. In this sense, the story of Bloodborne is very open as everyone has their own explanation: this is just how I understand from the incomplete information provided.

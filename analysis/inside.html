INSIDE: Design & Visual
INSIDE is a puzzle-platformer adventure game developed and published by Playdead in 2016.
time spring 2019
design and narrative
A fly ascends to the skies, pondering the pointlessness of its brief existence.
The sentence describes the story of The Plan. You play as a fly and all you can do is controlling where it goes. The reason I would rather call it "experience" is that it lacks some of the elements that make up a game: there is no goal in the game despite of the existence of an ending, and players have no challenges at all: they could just press one key and that is all it takes to finish the game. But at an experiential level, The Plan also opens many topics worth having deeper discussion. SPOILERS AHEAD
story
simplicity
You are a fly trying to go up.
That is what The Plan tells you, making it the simplest story ever in video games. It is worth noting that the story here doesn't necessarily match what the players comprehend and imagine. And by the graphics players are able to know the story at the very beginning, making it super clear to know what they should do.
ending
As you fly higher and higher, the game gradually zooms out the camera, rendering the fly smaller and smaller. And here comes the music, Peer Gynt Suite No. 1, Op. 46: II. The Death of Aase, and there is light shining up high. The orchestra grows louder as the fly ascends. And all of a sudden the fly touches a light bulb and gets killed immediately. We don't even know that bright light is emitted by the bulb before the game ends so it is really kind of surprising. And that's it: the game has no reward, or replayability.
openness
The ultimate simple story and the dramatic ending just do a excellent job delivering the higher concept of the game. The game is very open for understanding: some say it's an experimental deliberation on the meaning of life, some say it praises the courage to pursue and explore. The game is just like good literature that leaves space for players to think about, not just instilling the developer's idea, so that everyone gets some inspiration from it.
interaction
simplicity
The game's interaction is extremely simple as well: holding a key is good enough to finish the game. Just as mentioned above, this weakens the feeling that The Plan is a game; but on the other hand, who actually cares whether it is a game?
Graphics
The graphics in INSIDE is impressive - it keeps Limbo's dark ambience, but with the transition from black-white to fully color graphics, the game has much stronger feeling of a dystopian world.
color
desaturated world
Everything in INSIDE has less saturated color than normal, and this feels like we are seeing the world through fog, which matches feeling of a dystopian world.

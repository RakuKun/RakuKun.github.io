INSIDE: Design & Visual
INSIDE is a puzzle-platformer adventure game developed and published by Playdead in 2016. The highly stylish graphics makes this small game feel emotional, and its design especially narrative part works well in telling a dystopian story without words.
time spring 2019
Design and Narrative
INSIDE is more than just a puzzle-platformer in ways that it has a complete story. SPOILERS AHEAD
puzzle design
simple scene
Each scene in INSIDE is connected by puzzles, usually at most one puzzle for one scene. So the puzzle in form is not connected, and as the result each is not very complicated. INSIDE makes its puzzles feel "connected" with the continuity of the world and items - like the submarine in underwater levels. types of puzzle
Generally there are two prospective to start thinking of puzzles: making difficult one that offers unlimited time for thinking, and simpler one that requires correct input under time limitation. The two kinds of puzzles could balance the game difficulty and serve the narrative: when the character faces a puzzle where no one is here, it could be rather large scaled and complicated. If he is being chased by a hound, the puzzle is more like an adventure as time for reaction is short as he keeps running. After all, puzzle design serves the story. If it is annoyingly hard the story flow will be interrupted.
narrative
storytelling
Almost a direct contrast to text-rich game like Firewatch, INSIDE tells the story using only environment. The approach is suitable for players who likes the openness of the narrative, but not those who prefer a clear explanation - all except developers can only guess what the story is, but probably never 100% clear. pace
INSIDE keeps its pace balanced by adding transition scenes between puzzles: the character would walk a little bit after solving a puzzle and before running into a new one. Like mentioned above, time constrained puzzles offers more nervosity while unlimited time puzzle is a good chance to calm down
Graphics
The graphics in INSIDE is impressive - it keeps Limbo's dark ambience, but with the transition from black-white to fully colored world, the game has much stronger feeling of a dystopian world.
color
desaturated world
Everything in INSIDE has less saturated color than normal, and this feels like we are seeing the world through fog, which matches feeling of a dystopian world.
shape
Everything in INSIDE is not complicated in shape, what makes it feel good to watch is the good use of contrast and pattern.
contrast
No games before have a fly as the playable protagonist, and it proves to be a very wise choice.
pattern
Pattern is the group of shapes.

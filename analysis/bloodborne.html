Bloodborne Analysis
Bloodborne is an action role-playing game developed by FromSoftware and published by Sony Computer Entertainment exclusively for PlayStation 4. It is by far the most admired game for me - for its levels, narrative, and art. Built in a way similar to other Soul games, Bloodborne does have some special points that makes the game different. This analysis will focus on Bloodborne's design (level design, system design) and its narrative.
tempo winter 2019
Design
A fly ascends to the skies, pondering the pointlessness of its brief existence. The sentence describes the story of The Plan just as it is. You play as a fly and all you can do is controlling its flying direction. The reason I would rather call it "experience" is that it lacks some of the elements that make up a game: there is no goal in the game despite the existence of an ending, and players face no challenges at all: they could just press one key and that is all it takes to finish the game. But at a experience level, The Plan also opens many topics worth having deeper discussion. SPOILERS AHEAD
story
simplicity
You are a fly trying to go up.
That is what The Plan tells you, making it the simplest story ever in video games. It is worth noting that the story here doesn't necessarily match what the players comprehend and imagine. And by the graphics players are able to know the story at the very beginning, making it super clear to know what they should do.
ending
As you fly higher and higher, the game gradually zooms out the camera, rendering the fly smaller and smaller. And here comes the music, Peer Gynt Suite No. 1, Op. 46: II. The Death of Aase, and there is light shining up high. The orcheatra grows louder as the fly ascends. And all of a sudden the fly touches a light bulb and gets killed immediately. We don't even know that bright light is emitted by the bulb before the game ends so it is really kind of surprising. And that's it: the game has no reward, or replayability.
openness
The ultimate simple story and the dramatic ending just do a excellent job delivering the higher concept of the game. The game is very open for understanding: some say it's an experimental deliberation on the meaning of life, some say it praises the courage to pursue and explore. The game is just like good literature that leaves space for players to think about, not just instilling the developer's idea, so that everyone gets some inspiration from it.
interaction
simplicty
The game's interaction is extremely simple as well: holding a key is good enough to finish the game. Just as mentioned above, this weakens the feeling that The Plan is a game; but on the other hand, who actually cares whether it is a game?
Narrative
Like other titles of Soul series, Bloodborne has very stylized From Software's way of storytelling and narrative design.

world
The Night, and the Dream, were long...
Bloodborne's story is so complicated that even till today there is no universally acknowledged explaination for many details. But it is clear and people agree that its world background is constructed upon Cthulhu Mythos and there are many new settings. This game takes place in Yharnam, a city filled with diseases, blood and death. The city is plagued with a local disease which transforms the patients into bestial creatures. The player becomes one of the Hunters and hunts down those horrifying creatures while exploring the mysterious world of Bloodborne.
theme
In my perspective, the theme of Bloodborne is mankind's evolution.

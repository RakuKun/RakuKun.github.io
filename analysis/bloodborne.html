Bloodborne Analysis
Bloodborne is an action role-playing game developed by FromSoftware and published by Sony Computer Entertainment exclusively for PlayStation 4. It is by far the most admired game for me - for its levels, narrative, and art. Built in a way similar to other Soul games, Bloodborne does have some special points that makes the game different. This analysis will focus on Bloodborne's design (level design, system design) and its narrative.
time winter 2019
Design
As a game designer, I find in Bloodborne too many things to learn from: game mechanism, storytelling, world settings, and procedually generated Chalice Dungeons. Of course, many games have very great parts, too, but I still prefer Bloodborne most. In my opinion, the difference between being "great" and "marvelous" is the ability to internally unite design elements: to make everything work well together is more important than having a bunch of crazy ideas. Bloodborne does great in this aspect. In this part I will be talking mainly about their design approach. SPOILERS AHEAD
level design
simplicity
Just like the soul series made by From Software, the level of Bloodborne is beautiful and challenging as well. Waking up in a vast world with few hints for where to go next already seems hard enough for new players, let alone there are numbers of monsters along the way. But we do have some good news. The structure of Bloodborne's level is quite linear at the beginning and will only grow into branches once players get the hang of the game. And whatever players do, they will eventually come to a point where they need to face the first progress boss. It is almost unavoidable that many deaths are to come, but that will be a very important lesson for players to accept failures and get familiar with the map. It is just fair that players use life - usually not very valuable in the early game - to learn some basic rules and routines. As the story moves on, the game offers more options to choose from and as players have better equipment, higher level and more battle experience, it will not be so overwhelming. The game in this way ask "politely" players to think carefully and choose a route they feel comfortable, leaving space to choose for both new players and veterans.
level's functions
The levels have functions. One crucial thing for Bloodborne's map is the shortcut which drastically reduces the time needed to travel between places and it serves well. First, the designers of Bloodborne use the shortcut to ease the negative feeling of death. The shortcut is a small goal for players to challenge themselves to prove deaths not in vain. if the player opens a shortcut during the adventure, even if he/she dies he/she would not feel too depressed because next time the player can travel the world more efficiently. This design as well in the experience aspect intrigues player's curiosity and keeps their eyes open. Secondly, From Software decompose the large world into smaller areas and use shortcuts to connect them all, making them feel larger than they really are. Doors, hidden ladders, stairways just make Bloodborne a real 3-dimensional world connecting the lower world and higher places. Bloodborne makes players "WOW" when they take a long journey and climb a ladder just to find themselves in front of their wake up location! What is more, developers still hold control of the level design as the actions character can do are relatively limited - no jumping, only dash jumping when triggered. So designers can make precise prediction about where players are going so the work is highly goal-oriented and well controlled.
openness
Not every game has that elaborately polished map design - I would rather call it the genius gift - it is not meaningful to analyze the map structure without putting it under the need of art style and storytelling.
system design
combat system
It is hard to discuss Bloodborne's combat system without comparing it to Souls series as they share almost identical combat designing. But there is difference which shapes what is called Bloodborne, not "Dark Souls Victoria". In the game, Gothic buildings, people's clothing and firearms indicate that Bloodborne is based on a Victorian world, a time of technology innovation and city lores. Bloodborne offers a much faster combat pace for both player and enemy. There is only one wooden shield available for blocking, so in most cases it is better to parry and dodge to avoid taking damage. Firearms are most suitable to parry enemy's attack, making them stagger and vulnerable to deadly counter-attacks. To compliment the faster-paced combat and keep the action fast and furious, players can regain some of their lost health if they successfully performs counter-attacks. All the designs in combat system are in wonderful accordance, and highlight the theme of Bloodborne - the lust for blood. We encounter powerful enemies, get killed by them, kill them back, and finally the hunter outfit is stained with much blood.
Narrative
Like other titles of Soul series, Bloodborne has very stylized From Software's way of storytelling and narrative design.
story
world
The Night, and the Dream, were long...
Bloodborne's story is so complicated that even till today there is no universally acknowledged explaination for many details. But it is clear and people agree that its world background is constructed upon Cthulhu Mythos and there are many new settings. This game takes place in Yharnam, a city filled with diseases, blood and death. The city is plagued with a local disease which transforms the patients into bestial creatures. The player becomes one of the Hunters and hunts down those horrifying creatures while exploring the mysterious world of Bloodborne.
theme
In my perspective, the theme of Bloodborne is mankind's evolution.

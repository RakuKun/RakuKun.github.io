The Plan: An Experiment
The Plan is a 5-minute-long experience by Krillbite Studio. It is so short and special that I can hardly find a similar one to compare - it is more like a visual novel with a little interaction. On Steam its review has remained "Overwhelmingly Positive" since the release and most comments talk about their feeling and thoughts after playing. This game absolutely has some magic to attract so many players in just 5 minutes.
time summer 2019
Walkthrough
A fly ascends to the skies, pondering the pointlessness of its brief existence. The story of The Plan is all in this sentence. You play as a fly and all you can do is controlling the flying direction. The reason I would rather call it "experience" is that it lacks some of the elements that make up a game: there is no goal in the game despite the existence of an ending, and players face no challenges at all: they could just press one key and that is all it takes to finish the game. But at a experience level, The Plan also opens many topics worth having deeper discussion. SPOILERS
Story
story
The Plan probably has the simplest story ever in video games. Basically the player experience the short of life a fly. There is a beginning, some obstacles and overcoming (wind and spider web), but all these are not strong enough for a compelling experience yet.
As the fly goes higher and higher, the camera gradually zooms out, rendering the fly tiny and unnoticeable. Here is how the game starts to get fascinating. In the background a piece of classical music, Peer Gynt Suite No. 1, Op. 46: II. The Death of Aase by Edvard Grieg, grows louder, and the picture changes from real world scenes to metaphor.
Everything just feels so good: who ever has imagined a fly could reach stars and galaxy? It feels like the fly is reaching the heaven. The orchestra finally rises to the crescendo.
And all of a sudden the fly touches a light bulb and gets killed immediately, by which game's description is realized. A fly is just a fly, after all. What a great irony!
narrative
Though the game has no reward system or replayability, it does engage the players in a narrative way: giving continues positive feedback. The fly is always going up, the scene is changing, and bizarre things are happening. The game is a non-stop experience with the climate in the very end.
Using positive feedback is helpful for short experiences where player's expectation is easier to satisfy. However, for large games that has ups and downs like movies, designers must be careful because the pace should be changed to let players calm down and take a rest.
openness
The ultimately simple story and the dramatic ending just do a excellent job delivering the higher concept of the game. The game is very open for understanding: some say it's an experimental deliberation on the meaning of life, some say it praises the courage to pursue and explore. The game is just like good literature that leaves space for players to think about, not just instilling the developer's idea, so that everyone gets some inspiration from it.
Interaction
simplicity
The game's interaction is extremely simple as well: holding a key is good enough to finish the game. Just as mentioned above, this weakens the feeling that The Plan is a game; but on the other hand, who actually cares whether it is a game?
Inspiration
The Plan inspires me in many ways.
Openness
An open story welcomes vast possibilities. Using rhetoric ways, like metaphor works well letting the story speak for itself, and gives freedom to players to understand. Simpler in form, more openness and complexity in content.
Bizarreness
No games before have a fly as the playable protagonist, and it proves to be a very wise choice. In a fly's perspective which no one has probably tried before, everything happening is more understandable and as audience we are more likely to be open for changes: after all, it is just a fly, there is nothing important about it, just show me what you imagine.

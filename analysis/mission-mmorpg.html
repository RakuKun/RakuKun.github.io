Creating Missions for MMORPG
I worked during the summer vacation of 2018 as a game design intern for Revelation Online Mobile, an MMORPG developed by Pangu Studio of NetEase. Under the supervision of lead designer and a system designer fellow, I created several side missions with a focus on fun features that would make players feel really immersed in the world. The ideal side missions don’t have to be very long yet the innovation of gameplay and storytelling is highly valued.
time summer 2018
platform Unity
work quest design, level design
Methodology
Before setting off the production, it is very important to have an overview of what the game ‘feels like’ and how we deliver side missions. It is a high fantasy world with many imaginary creatures and magic.
system overview
adventure system
Adventure System is a collection of gameplay, content and reward. It aims to encourage players to explore the game by providing nice single player focused experience. Most missions in Adventure System are not compulsory, about 5 minutes long, and challenging but also rewarding.
transformation system
Transformation System is a very special one by which character may transform into another species in the game, replacing the models, animations, skills, even the camp with the new one. For instance, if a character becomes a crab, they walk sideways, use claws to attack, and become friends with other NPC crabs. The system grants designers brand new ways to think about how living things in the game interact with each other, and how to implement unforgettable experience using it.
camera system
Photography System is not a rare system in MMO games, but the one in Revelation has some cool features such as capturing ghost creatures and recognizing NPC. This opens opportunity to implement it into missions.
audio system
There is a special item to play sounds in the game, and player collects sound under the framework of Audio System. It offers interaction with the sound, such as affecting some creatures, adding buff, and triggering events.
design principle
combine systems
My mentor and I both agree that the most fun comes from the cross-product of multiple systems. There are 4 special ones listed above, and of course many basic systems (economy, battle, mission, achievement) are so fundamental that we cannot go without them.
One of the practical ways is to think of the components of gameplay and try finding some possible options for each. The formula is:
Interaction Function * Entity * (Other) = Complete Gameplay
reward
It is better to reward players for exploring Adventure System as the missions are additional and optional. We decided to use a unique currency (called "Rainbow Jade") for missions so it won't affect in-game economic balance and is easier to determine the price of items.
Production
During the internship I analysed existing gameplay and created many new ones.
ideation
problems of former ones
Before I joined, the team already had some prototype design for Adventure System. After playing all of them, I did sense the disconnection between the quests and the world. Here is my analysis.
improvement direction
I managed to figure out what is the right track of fixing the problem and creating new side missions. For the old ones, I try to find ways to put them in a larger context (to fit the backstory, to combine more systems, to be more challening and rewarding) so the core fun of Adventure System is well reflected in those missions. For the new ones, I stick to the design principles concluded in Methodology session.
mission examples
Crab Wars
Side mission: Crab Wars lets players transform into a crab and go through a bizarre experience in fighting with and against crabs. This mission fully uses the Transform System and uses trick in switching camps.
Mission Procedure: 1. Character (as human) notices some blue crabs fighting with red ones. 2. If the character uses transformation tool to transform into a crab with either color, they can talk to the crab commander and choose to fight for their species. 3. After killing some enemy crabs the character can talk to the commander again to finish the mission and get reward from crab's gratitude.
The interesting part in gameplay is that the player could actually do mission 2 times for the both team, and have different conversation and receive achievement. I think this mission does a good job in encouraging players to explore the world, and crabs fighting is an interesting and rare point so transforming and getting a mission meets player's curiosity.
Unseen Love
Side mission: Unseen Love tells a story between a woman and her ghost lover using the feature of Camera System.
Mission Procedure: 1. Character wanders on the street and finds a weeping woman under a peach tree. 2. As the character talks to the woman, she tells that she's missing her dead husband. She has a feeling that her husband is still around there. 3. If the character uses camera, they will find a ghost man under the peach tree, looking at the woman. The ghost says that he's been dead for some time and even his ghost figure is fading away, and he wants the woman to live happy without her. 4. If the character takes a photo of the man and shows to the woman, the woman get relieved as she knows now he's always here with her.
I spent some time researching the love story between human and ghost and designed this mission using the Camera System that allows the ghost to appear in frame. I want to use dialogue clues as indirect control for those who are eager to solve problems. The most surprising moment should be turning on the camera to see a person right in front of you!

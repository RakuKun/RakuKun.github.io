Creating Missions for MMORPG
I worked during the summer vacation of 2018 as a game design intern for Revelation Online Mobile, an MMORPG developed by Pangu Studio of NetEase. Under supervision of the lead designer and a system designer mentor, I created several side quests focusing on the narrative and gameplay that would make players feel immersed in the high fantasy world.
time summer 2018
platform Unity
work quest design, level design
Methodology
Before setting off on the adventure, it is very important to have an overview of what the game ‘feels like’ and how we should deliver side missions. Revelation's backstory is pretty much like the extension of traditional Chinese legend: Classic of Mountains and Seas (Shanhaijing), a high fantasy world with many imaginary creatures. This means a large scale of possibility to implement new gameplay under a more conservative theme.
Since the game has already gone through 5 iteration cycle by the time I joined, I was able to concentrate on design with a fairly complete system framework.
system overview
System in MMORPG context refers to the framework that takes charge of a certain gameplay. Revelation contains a bunch of systems ranging from very basic ones such Character Growth, Equipment, Mission (just as other MMORPGs do) to more specific ones like Adventure, Transformation and Camera. The following are those that I mainly worked on.
Adventure System
I mostly worked in creating content for Adventure System, which provides unique experience to amplify the feeling of "freedom" and "depth". It aims to encourage players to explore the world by providing engaging single-player focused experience. Missions in Adventure System are not compulsory and are designed for those who like to spend more time in game.
Transformation System
Transformation System allows character to transform into another creature in the game by replacing their models, animations, skills, even the faction. For instance, if a character becomes a crab, they walk sideways, use claws to attack, and become friends with other AI crabs. The system grants designers brand new ways to think about how living things in the game interact with each other, and inspires me to implement an unforgettable experience.
Camera System
Camera System is not a rare one in MMO games, but the one in Revelation has some cool features such as capturing ghost creatures (that are unseen normally) and recognizing the name of anything in the frame.
Audio System
Player can collect some sound and use a special item to play sounds thanks to Audio System. It is special in a way that it records the sound ID thus allowing triggering events that have sounds as parameters, which means more options to choose from when designing side quests.
design principle
combining systems
My mentor and I both agree that the most fun comes from the crosspoint of multiple systems. There are 4 special ones listed above, and of course many basic systems (economy, battle, mission, achievement) are also open to offer fundamental gameplay.
After getting familiar with systems, I figured out a pratical way to create quests: observe the components of gameplay and try finding some possible options for each. The formula is:
Interactive Feature * Entity * Fantasy * Reward = Complete Mission.
Interactive feature comes from the functions that game system could offer. Entity is the subject (usually the player) and object of the interaction. Fantasy is the narrative logic behind the interaction: why is it happening and why the player should take the challenge? And reward is of course, what you will get.

reward
Players need feedback and reward for exploring Adventure System and doing optional quests. We decided to use a unique currency (called "Rainbow Jade") only for buying items in Adventure System so it won't affect in-game economic balance and is easier to determine the price of items.
Production
When it comes to production, my major work was filling Excel sheets and adjusting things directly in Unity. I created side quests, did rounds of debugging and iteration, and helped organize playtests with naive guests.
ideation
problems of former ones
Before I joined, the team already had some prototype design for Adventure System. After playing all of them, I did sense the disconnection between the quests and the world. Here is my analysis.
improvement direction
I managed to figure out what is the right track of fixing the problem and creating new side missions. For the old ones, I try to find ways to put them in a larger context (to fit the backstory, to combine more systems, to be more challening and rewarding) so the core fun of Adventure System is well reflected in those missions. For the new ones, I stick to the design principles concluded in Methodology session.
mission examples
Crab Wars
Side mission: Crab Wars lets players transform into a crab and go through a bizarre experience in fighting with and against crabs. This mission fully uses the Transform System and uses trick in switching factions.
Mission Procedure: 1. Character (as human) notices some blue crabs fighting with red ones. 2. If the character uses transformation tool to transform into a crab with either color, they can talk to the crab commander and choose to fight for their species. 3. After killing some enemy crabs the character can talk to the commander again to finish the mission and get reward from crab's gratitude.
The interesting part in gameplay is that the player could actually do mission 2 times for the both team, and have different conversation and receive achievement. I think this mission does a good job in encouraging players to explore the world, and crabs fighting is an interesting and rare point so transforming and getting a mission meets player's curiosity.
Unseen Love
Side mission: Unseen Love tells a story between a woman and her ghost lover using the feature of Camera System.
Mission Procedure: 1. Character wanders on the street and finds a weeping woman under a peach tree. 2. As the character talks to the woman, she tells that she's missing her dead husband. She has a feeling that her husband is still around there. 3. If the character uses camera, they will find a ghost man under the peach tree, looking at the woman. The ghost says that he's been dead for some time and even his ghost figure is fading away, and he wants the woman to live happy without her. 4. If the character takes a photo of the man and shows to the woman, the woman get relieved as she knows now he's always here with her.
I spent some time researching the love story between human and ghost and designed this mission using the Camera System that allows the ghost to appear in frame. I want to use dialogue clues as indirect control for those who are eager to solve problems. The most surprising moment should be turning on the camera to see a person right in front of you!

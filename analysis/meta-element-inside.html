"Meta-game" in Inside English Chinese
 First released in 2016, Inside is an incredible side-scrolling game that explores further and deeper in almost every aspect than developer’s previous critically claimed title Limbo. What interests me most, above its marvelous graphics and intriguing story, is designer’s skill of combining levels and meta- game element for the unique art of storytelling.

 In so-called “hidden ending” the boy in the game pulls the plug of mind controlling device and felt to uncontrollable, which is often explained that the character gets out of player’s control as he manages to cut the relationship inside and outside the game (of course this is only one of the possibilities). This kind of gameplay is generally referred to “meta-game”, a concept that went heated in recent years. And since this word came to people’s eyes, it has been a novel, even selling point of a game. The interesting point of meta-game is the feeling of breaking the routine: the story may not go as you think it should be – something in the game may try to break the connection of storyline; the same applies to the character, the game objects and scenes. This skill is efficient as it solves the conflict between player interaction and storytelling. However, when more and more games use similar method, the meta-games seem to be less appealing as players begin to get familiar with the routine and even predict what will happen next. This shows the fact that “meta-game” is not beyond game: it is only a method that adds to the variety of game genres.

 What Inside does that excels some others, however, is the story and gameplay bound to it making Inside more of a “meta-game”. In the game there is no conversation, no narration, no explicit cultural symbols. Even that “hidden” ending does not tell anything related to the world setting. So how do we players make a “meta-game” guessing? Inside makes its “hidden” ending surprising but relatively understandable: it is based on the fact that players have all gone through the “normal” ending and the game puts forward merely an alternative option for the players. The two endings are not in conflict but explain each other respectively. We consider the “hidden” ending to have meta element as it seems to indicate the relationship between player and game, and we guess this relationship exists because the scenes, the game objects support our reasoning. We tend to think the character has cut the connection between him and player because in the game, many walking-deads are also under the charge of mind controlling devices – the reasoning is within game context. In a word, the meta element is the reasoning result of our gameplay experience, not the condition. The “meta” element Inside contains appears to be a rather inner part of its serious topic. The designers add difficulties for finding the “hidden” ending so we hardly discover the fact that we manipulate the life and death of the little boy. That is, in my opinion, more successful than using meta element to add fun or novelty to the game.

 What is more interesting, though, is the fact that the condition of reaching the hidden ending is so challenging that few people (at least no one around me!) are able to achieve without googling for a walk-through. Players seek help merely for the hidden performance, for some experience they do not have the ability to get on their own. Does this further expand the definition of a meta-game: to influence the player help complete the game? I do not know, because by this definition it seems the range meta-game can cover is too broad. Inside is an interesting game because it provides with various aspect for players to talk about, and “meta” is only one of them which shows the possibility the designers can use to help tell a better story and form a thicker repressed atmosphere.
这篇文章是对Inside中“元游戏”的分析。

Inside 是Playdead制作室在2016年发售的一款横板解密类游戏。众所周知，游戏与其他娱乐媒体的互动方式最显着的许多不同之处，就在各种玩法表现出来的高度互动性。这本来是游戏的一大优势，合理的玩法规划能令玩家更简单沉溺游戏之中；但若一款游戏的制造意图并非单纯为了文娱，而过多的阻止玩家的自由，单纯想以叙事交代剧情或表达作者意图为主的话，高度的互动性就可能对游戏形成阻止。 我们不妨先想象一个场景：当游戏进行到必定阶段，需求一个NPC交互剧情的触发点，你是站在原地静静听他说完感受剧情，或是四处走动干点别的事？相信大多数玩家毫无疑问的都会挑选后者（比如这时候你会收集材料，整理装备...）。不过考虑到游戏的剧情和布景，玩家这些行为往往都是不符合当时环境和角色定位的；于是这段预先规划好的剧情效果，不光没有让玩家代入剧情，反而让玩家产生了“出戏”的效果，彻底与制作者的预期的想法背道而驰了。（或者这就是为什么我们喜欢小岛秀夫的电影式游戏的原因，玩主线时，代入感非常强！）

因而现在大多数游戏在需求讲述剧情的时分，往往会挑选削减场景内可互动的要素、乃至完全限制玩家的行动的方法来凸显剧情；当玩家无事可做的时分，就不得不把注意力更多地会集到剧情上面了（其间做得最完全的就是《暴雨》和《超凡双生》）。但这种做法也有弊端——假如互动仅为触发叙事而存在，这样的作品在严厉意义上还能称之为游戏吗？ 再说下去就有点违背主题了。总而言之，叙事与玩法的对立一直是困扰业界的严重难题，不过元游戏好像为此找到了一条特殊的解决之道。由于元游戏自身就要故意露出自己的虚拟特点，所以一般游戏只怕避之不及的荒诞和出戏，反而是元游戏求之不得的手法。 以年头的一款元游戏《小马岛（pony island）》为例。无论是呈现的开端界面，仍是无聊到爆的pony island（游戏里的小马跑步游戏），其实处处都在提醒玩家“这仅仅个游戏”。但玩过的都知道，在小马的国际之外其实还存在着别的一层由撒旦控制的国际，这个国际才是玩家的主战场。因而所谓的出戏并没有真实的出戏，仅仅为了另一部戏做铺垫算了 一起《小马岛》还经过各式各样的手法暗示玩家：我（游戏）知道在这个游戏国际之外，还存在别的着一个国际（既玩家地点的国际）。这时分，连玩家地点的实际国际也被归入游戏傍边；玩家不再需求寄予于某个人物参加游戏，而是真实成为了游戏的参加者之一，天然也不存在出戏一说了。

我想初次尝试元游戏的玩家，一定会被它打破常规的构思所惊倒，但这个长处从另一视点来说，却是元游戏的危险地点。现在元游戏的趣味主要源自于打破知识的意外感——这在《小马岛》上体现得适当显着。如果有一天，咱们早已对元游戏的各种套路司空见惯，不再对它感到惊讶的时分，还能从中取得趣味吗？ 从这个视点考虑元游戏的未来，好像是走进了一个死胡同：元游戏的开展必定伴随着它的盛行，但盛行的一起又会带来玩家的审美疲劳。如此看来，元游戏好像只能是稍纵即逝了。 好在现实并非如此。在文学范畴，前人早已为咱们指明晰道路。元小说其实早已在现代小说中占有重要位置，它呈现的原因并非单纯地来自于小说关于创始新形式的需求；一起元小说在体现某些题材上，比较传统的小说的确有其不行替代的优势。

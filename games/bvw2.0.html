Building Virtual Worlds
Building Virtual Worlds is a class for the first semester of Master of Entertainment Technology Program during which I in a team of five take part as game designer and artist to make quick game prototypes. The process takes one to three weeks.
time fall 2019
work game design, art, programming
Water Truckin': Evil Hero
Water Truckin': Evil Hero is a 2v2 multiplayer game on Jam-O-Drum, a device that allows 4 players to play around a table with a projector.
time one week
theme fun
platform Jam-O-Drum
work game design, environmental art, UI and island collapse animation
design elements
asymmetrical role
One player drives the firetruck, another controls the water cannon. By assigning different abilities we make players stay connected to each other and communicate along the game.
unavoidable conflict
It is supposed to be a fast paced game no longer than 2 minutes each round. The battleground gets smaller as the game goes on, players will have to fight more intensely. Reasonable randomness. For competitive games people are expecting each round to be different (and the result as well). It’s not good to be 100% skill or 100% luck, so adding a little randomness will help the game gets fun. The randomness must be well controlled to not overwhelm players’ performance.
visual instructions
Tell the players what will happen in game and make them understand the situation.
reflection
clearer instructions
There is almost no tutorial so we have to tell players manually how to play and win the game.
balance
Most players claim that driving is actually harder than spraying water. It is not a bad thing to have a diverse job difficulty inside a team, but we need to clarify it in the first place or players might get frustrated for underperforming.
Promposal
Promposal, combined with Prompt and Proposal, is a VR game where player experiences a classroom romance by a sequence of interactive stories.
time two weeks
theme interactive story
platform Oculus Rift S
work story, game design, all characters' models and animations
design elements
easy story with ups and downs
It will be a 4 minute experience including interaction time so do not make the story too complicated. We go for a classroom romance since everyone probably has some experience on that. And we think adding a little surprise in story would help with the emotional flow.
meaningful interactions
We learn from What Remains of Edith Finch combining interactions with story. We leave important moments waiting to be accomplished by player interaction so they feel being in the situation rather than watching a story sequence.
design process
idea iteration
We spent much time iterating each story and interaction.
reflection
deliver more clearly
Some players have a hard time figuring out how the story and interaction is connected. And it is hard to have the same feeling as What Remains of Edith Finch as we are short on art and sound resources. So it might be better if we deliver the interaction in a clearer way with less metaphors.
do playtest as possible
More playtesters help more in finding bugs and testing the interactions. If they don’t understand, that is probably we are making it too complicated.
Paramania
Paramania is a puzzle-solving game where two players take on an adventure inside the Pyramid created by an evil Pharaoh.
time three weeks
theme ETC festival
platform CAVE (cave automatic virtual environment).
work puzzle design, environmental art and effects, playtests.
design elements
think of hardware
We are putting players into a very special game environment where they are surrounded by three large screens so they are like ‘inside’ the game world. This is an excellent chance for us to make a fully immersive game so we start the exploration finding concept for a Egyptian pyramid where adventurers go and find treasures by solving puzzles.
flow experience
At the early stage the game merely consists of multiple puzzles with little transition between each other, which for most players could be tedious. I then suggested that we have pre-scripted animations between two puzzles so the experience will have its relaxed and intense part. That is also how we implement story into the actual game.
unified theme
At first we have many ideas about puzzles and they all seem to work in some way. But after several playtests we find that have just one unified theme for all puzzles will make it clearer for players and easier to control the difficulty for designers. We decided on "light" as our core element as it is COOL and uses CAVE environment in a smart way.
design process
idea iteration
We begin with thinking some possible interesting mechanism that could utilize the specialty of CAVE, they are: light reflection and refraction, matching patterns, triggering with flashlight, and drawing. Our first playable prototype was a mixture of different kinds of puzzles but later we all agreed that "light reflection" is the most suitable mechanism.
reflection
playtests!
We have some really nice puzzles and our environment really has Egyptian feeling, but that doesn’t work very well if the experience is way too long. We have a 9 minute walkthrough which exceeds the expected 6 minutes much. It turns out we still need more playtests as some players may need more time to understand and play with the mechanics.

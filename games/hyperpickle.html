Project HyperPickle (WIP)
HyperPickle is a client project built for picoCTF 2021, a two-week online cybersecurity competition. The team is building a game that's oriented around delivering a fun, engaging experience to attract people to join the competition. The game features the ability to exchange resources and make friends with aliens while exploring the interesting, diverse cosmos.

I am working with: JD He, Andrew McLamb, Aiden Sun, Evelyn Sun, and Muzi Wang.

Check our project website.

Challenges and Solutions
This game is designed primarily for middle school and high school students with little to intermediate knowledge in cybersecurity area. The lack of knowledge required necessarily to answer picoCTF questions used to be a major setback when progressing the game that was dependent on such questions.

To address the solution, I present HyperPickle, a game that's oriented around levels of progression, unique achievements and a space theme to engage and encourage the contestants to answer questions with a high entertainment value.
Background
PicoCTF is a annual two-week cybersecurity online competition held by the CMU CyLab. The competition has been popular among middle school to college students across the globe, and the collaboration between the ETC and CyLab has been successfully running for years with a game built each year for the competition. Again, the same goal was assigned to our team, but the difference is that we managed to dig further into client's requirements.
Pre-production
The team has spent the first four weeks in the semester understanding the project and getting prepared for full-production.

understanding the goal
The salient point of working on a delivery project is to know the target audience and understand the client’s requirements, so as to set goals accordingly. During the communication, we got to know that the majority of contestants who try the game will be middle to high school students with little to intermediate cybersecurity knowledge, and our client wanted the following: to engage them in the game, encourage them to answer different challenges, and entertain them with fun, positive content. We will not be, however, making an education game, or creating the challenges ourselves.
past projects
The past picoCTF projects are great learning resources and the team all played the 2019 game which is the only one then-available.

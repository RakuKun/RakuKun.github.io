Project Google Stadia (ongoing)
In March 2019 Google Stadia announced a platform exclusive feature called State Share. This technology leverages cloud game hosting to enable players to share a complete game state (not just a specified save state) instantly to their friends or to the public. This project explores the affordances of this technology specifically for the discovery of new kinds of game design patterns and new game genres that are inherently viral and require elaborate player cooperation across massive scale audiences.
I work in a team of five at Entertainment Technology Center Silicon Valley. The project is still under development and more content will be added on a quite regular basis.

Ideation
Before the 1/4 walkaround we spent about three weeks doing research and thinking of new ideas. As State Share is a relatively new function exclusively developed for Stadia and with little reference or tech demo by far, we start off thinking the special points of State Share and how can it contribute to the game design and community.
research
special points
Before thinking how we build games based on State Share, we agreed that it is very important to determine the advantage of Google Stadia and especially State Share that we are working on. After reading the posts on developer website HERE, we concluded some merits that diffeciate State Share with traditional online or local multiplayer network.
1. State Share works well with Youtube streaming as it posts a link in the chat and audience can jump into the game simply by clicking the link. This enables new ways for streamers to interact with their audience. 2. State Share will record last 30s video before creating the link, and all information during that time will be included in the link. The developers have much freedom to decide which and how the information is to be used. 3. State Share will work with other Stadia features such as Crowd Play and Stadia Team is working to bring more flexibility to its function. In that sense, we as developers would concentrate on finding a well structured gameplay that manuplates State Share as its core mechanics.
possible usage
After internal and client meetings we concluded 10 possible ways to use State Share.
prototype
narrowing down

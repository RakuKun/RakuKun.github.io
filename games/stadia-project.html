Project Google Stadia (ongoing)
In March 2019 Google Stadia announced a platform exclusive feature called State Share. This technology leverages cloud game hosting to enable players to share a complete game state (not just a specified save state) instantly to their friends or to the public. This project explores the possibility of this technology specifically for the discovery of new kinds of game design patterns and new game genres that are inherently viral and require elaborate player cooperation across massive scale audiences.

I work in a team of five at Entertainment Technology Center Silicon Valley. The project is still under development and more content will be added on a quite regular basis.

The project website, which is hosted by the ETC, could be found HERE.The website is planned to post new blogs and findings every week.

Ideation
Before the 1/4 walkaround we spent about three weeks doing research and thinking of new ideas. As State Share is a relatively new function exclusively developed for Stadia and with little reference or tech demo so far, we start off thinking the special points of State Share and how can it contribute to the game design and community.
research
special points
Before thinking how we build games based on State Share, we agreed that it is very important to determine the advantage of Google Stadia and especially State Share that we are working on. After reading the posts on developer website HERE, we concluded some merits that differentiate State Share with traditional online or local multiplayer network.
1. State Share works well with Youtube streaming as it posts a link in the chat and audience can jump into the game simply by clicking the link. This enables new ways for streamers to interact with their audience.
2. State Share will record last 30s video before creating the link, and all information during that time will be included in the link. The developers have much freedom to decide which and how the information is to be used.
3. State Share will work with other Stadia features such as Crowd Play and Stadia Team is working to bring more flexibility to its function. In that sense, we as developers would concentrate on finding a well structured gameplay that manipulates State Share as its core mechanics.
possible usage
After internal and client meetings we concluded 10 possible ways to use State Share.
prototype
specification
Starting from week 3, we decided to narrow down ideas by eliminating those unpractical or not using State Share as a important mechanism. And this time we got 5 more concrete ideas that are ready for prototyping.
paper prototype
We use paper prototypes for the 5 ideas as designing on paper is easier technically and expose the core mechanics well. The one I designed explores the concept of "delivering the state".
It is also very important to run playtests as many as possible. For paper prototypes we have run 3 rounds of playtests, the first and second being internal testing, and the third time we invited some EA employees whose experience is very valuable to improve our design. Here are some suggestions from our playtesters:
1. Think of State Share as a core feature without which the gameplay would change a lot.
2. Be careful about the theme: is the fiction suitable for all ages? Will the game mechanism ask for some private information such as living location?
3. Online multiplayer games are challenging but seems more promising, while unsynchronized gameplay is easier to do digital prototypes.
Now since we have the quarter walkaround, we are stepping into the digital prototyping phase.

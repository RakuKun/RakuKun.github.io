Project Ditto for Stadia (ongoing)


I work in a team of five as game designer at Entertainment Technology Center Silicon Valley to develop a game for Stadia platform. The semester-long project is under firm development and more content will be added on a quite regular basis.

Please pay a visit at project website if interested in more details.
I also write design documentation for the project to record our process and design takeaways, check our online design documentation.
time spring 2020
platform Unity, Google Stadia
work game design, programming
Background
In March 2019 Google Stadia announced a platform exclusive feature called State Share. This technology leverages cloud game hosting to enable players to share a complete game state (not just a specified save state) instantly to their friends or to the public. The project explores the possibility of this technology specifically for the discovery of new kinds of game design patterns and new game genres that are inherently viral and require elaborate player cooperation across massive scale audiences.
Ideation
We spent about three weeks doing research and thinking of new ideas. As State Share is a relatively new function exclusively developed for Stadia with little reference or tech demo so far, we start off thinking the special points of State Share and how can it contribute to the game design and streaming community.
researching
special points
After reading the posts on the developer website, we concluded some merits that differentiate State Share with massive online multiplayer or local multiplayer feature.
1. State Share works well with Youtube streaming as it posts a link in the chat and audience can jump into the game simply by clicking the link. This enables new ways for streamers to interact with their audience.
2. State Share will record last 30s video before creating the link, and all information during that time will be included in the link. The developers have much freedom to decide which and how the information is to be used.
3. State Share will work with other Stadia features such as Crowd Play and Stadia Team is working to bring more flexibility to its function. In that sense, we as developers would concentrate on finding a well structured gameplay that manipulates State Share as its core mechanics.
possible usage
After internal and client meetings we concluded 10 possible ways to use State Share.
prototyping
specification
Starting from week 3, we decided to narrow down ideas by eliminating those unpractical or not using State Share as a important mechanism. And this time we got 5 more concrete ideas that are ready for prototyping.
paper prototype
We use paper prototypes for the 5 ideas as designing on paper is easier technically and could expose the core mechanics well. The one I designed explores the concept of "delivering the state".
It is also very important to run playtests as many as possible. For paper prototypes we have run 3 rounds of playtests, the first and second being internal testing, and the third time we invited some EA individuals whose experience is very valuable to improve our design. Here are some suggestions from the playtesters:
1. Think of State Share as a core feature without which the gameplay would change a lot.
2. Be careful about the theme: is the fiction suitable for all ages? Will the game mechanism ask for some private information such as living location?
3. Some of our paper prototypes have issues on replayability as they are puzzle driven. This can be difficult as State Share is designed for a relatively large number of people so spoilers may have an impact on the game.
4. Online multiplayer games are challenging but seems more promising, while unsynchronized gameplay is easier to do digital prototypes.
Now since we have done the quarter walkaround, we are stepping into the digital prototyping phase.
learnings
There are three criteria that could boost state sharing:
Asymmetrical gameplay is suitable for streamer and audience and well conveys State Share’s idea.
Having a persistent world (like mmo) is state sharable and could let players feel more immersed.
To make multiplicative distribution rather than transferring a single branch to new players.
digital prototype
Starting week 5 (March 9) the team will be working on making two digital prototypes till week 8. The first and the one we put major work on is Virus vs. Cells theme which utilizes State Share as the core mechanism: without State Share the game will be unplayable. The second one uses State Share as a way solely for exchanging information and game progress, so the mechanism could be more conservative. The main purpose for doing two is that we try to explore further in two directions of which we don't know for sure what will be the hit.
My major job is designing levels for the Virus one and program for the second one.
Production
The production phase is set to start after Week 8 (the halves presentation). We will begin the production work once we settle down on a concept that we all are interested and confident to build.

Multiplayer Combat Zone Design
Designing multiplayer combat experience has alway been a very interesting topic for me, so I want this page to cover some thoughts and trying of designing small-scale multiplayer combat zone, preferably 1v1 and 2v2.
time summer 2020
length 1 minute
software Unreal Engine 4, Maya, Illustrator
work individual work
Inspiration
I have been watching some top streamers on Twitch playing Call of Duty: Warzone quite a bit recently. Among many interesting game mechanics, the desperate 1v1 gunfight in a fictional gulag attracts me most for its dramatically fast-paced combat and the rule that only the winner can step back into the battlefield.
analysis
what's gulag?
As a Battle Royale game, Call of Duty: Warzone is praised for its fast-paced and Call-of-Duty-esque feeling of gunfight. A player is put into the gulag if and only after they get killed in the game for the first time, so gulag is a chance for everyone to push back to the main game - if they manage to win there.

A one versus one fight is prepared for everyone in the gulag, and some special rules apply: the system will pick a gun (or not!) randomly for both sides, and/or some equipments such as flash grenades or C4. The health does not regenerate here, and each round only has 40 seconds, with a "overtime" setting where players can also capture the flag in the middle of the map to win.
Here's the link to the official guide to gulag.
why gulag?
The gulag is designed, along with many other unique systems in Warzone, in order that players can MAXIMIZE their playtime in the game and keep excited. Similar to the main game where TTK (time to kill) is relatively short compared to other Battle Royale games, gunfights in gulag also try to make battle short and exciting. The higher the risk, the better the reward.

In the gulag map, many factors work successfully together to make the gunfight fast and intense. I want to discuss how designers achieve their goals by looking into the 2d and 3d layout of the map.
map: 2d
According to the official guide, the 1v1 fight can take place randomly at one of the following three maps.
From 2d layouts we can find the three maps share some things in common:
- Rigidly axial symmetric layout. It's probably the most important thing in any competitive games that both sides play fairly, and the most straightforward thing is make a symmetrical map so they play under the same circumstances.
- Single-floor and small map with lots of obstacles blocking the sight. From the purpose of gulag, we know that designers want this combat to be quick and decisive, which pushes the map to be simple and understandable as possible. But being simple does not means being easy: the players should be put into a challenging enough environment where they need to use everything they're provided to win.
- A perimeter balcony above where idle prisoners can observe the ground. No doubt this design gives advantage to the players who have teammates in the jail, which is a really interesting design to keep them away from boredom.
map: 3d
One very important design I found about the three gulag map is that, most walls are so TALL that players cannot peek upwards, but have to peek left/right, which adds to the odds they get killed.
This design again forces the players to take higher risks in the fight, by moving closer to the enemy or exposing a larger part of the body.
